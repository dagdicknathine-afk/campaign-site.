Deep Roots In Transcona

As a 22-year resident of Transcona and alumnus of Transcona Collegiate Institute, my perspective was shaped right here on our local blocks, at the East End Arena, and working community services.

For the last decade, I have served as a municipal frontline City Operator. I manage water distribution infrastructure, coordinate arena access, and understand the technical mechanics of our public works system from the ground up.

"I don't just sit in committee rooms analyzing structural policy—I've spent ten years executing it out on the streets. Let's cut the downtown red tape and get this work done right."

The Framework For Action

Infrastructure First

Transitioning from reactive pothole patching to a proactive, long-term "Sealant-First" pavement preservation program to protect our roads.

Community Safety

Empowering neighborhoods with the Guardian Camera Partnership, offering retroactive security camera rebates to prioritize local safety.

Green & Civic Space

Cutting application red tape with localized Horticultural Grants to fund clean soils, irrigation, and literacy programs for local garden clubs.

Frontline Efficiency

Utilizing clean-loop reverse vending installations in community spaces to reduce public littering and ease structural stress on maintenance crews.
